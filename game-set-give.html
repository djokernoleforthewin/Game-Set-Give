The Process

A simple act.
A lasting effect.

Used balls decompressed from play retain their shape and texture — exactly what anxious shelter dogs respond to. The chain from court to kennel takes less than a week.

01

After the match

When a ball loses its pressure and can no longer be used in competitive play, it is typically discarded. Instead, place it in any of our collection boxes at clubs and public courts across the city.

02

We collect and sort

Our volunteers collect from drop boxes weekly. Balls are cleaned and sorted before being dispatched to partner shelters. No ball goes to waste — even heavily worn ones are welcomed.

03

Shelters receive them

Rescue dogs, particularly those from difficult situations, benefit greatly from tactile objects. Tennis balls are soft, familiar, and stimulating — a small comfort with a measurable effect on wellbeing.
